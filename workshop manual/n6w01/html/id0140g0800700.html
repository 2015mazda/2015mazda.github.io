2014 - Mazda6 - Engine

MASS AIR FLOW (MAF) SENSOR INSPECTION [SKYACTIV-G 2.5]

Visual Inspection

1. Disconnect the negative battery cable. (See NEGATIVE BATTERY CABLE DISCONNECTION/CONNECTION [SKYACTIV-G 2.5].)
2. Disconnect the MAF sensor/IAT sensor No.1 connector.
3. Remove the MAF sensor/IAT sensor No.1. (See MASS AIR FLOW (MAF) SENSOR/INTAKE AIR TEMPERATURE (IAT) SENSOR NO.1 REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [SKYACTIV-G 2.5].)
4. Visually inspect the MAF sensor for the following:

  • Damage, cracks, dirt

  • Rusted sensor terminal

  • Bent sensor terminal

    • If there is any malfunction, repair or replace the MAF sensor/IAT sensor No.1. (See MASS AIR FLOW (MAF) SENSOR/INTAKE AIR TEMPERATURE (IAT) SENSOR NO.1 REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [SKYACTIV-G 2.5].)

Voltage Inspection

1. Disconnect the negative battery cable. (See NEGATIVE BATTERY CABLE DISCONNECTION/CONNECTION [SKYACTIV-G 2.5].)
2. Remove the MAF sensor/IAT sensor No.1 without disconnecting the connector. (See MASS AIR FLOW (MAF) SENSOR/INTAKE AIR TEMPERATURE (IAT) SENSOR NO.1 REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [SKYACTIV-G 2.5].)
3. Reconnect the negative battery cable. (See NEGATIVE BATTERY CABLE DISCONNECTION/CONNECTION [SKYACTIV-G 2.5].)
4. Connect the M-MDS to the DLC–2.
5. Switch the ignition ON (engine off).
6. As the air gradually approaches the MAF detection part of the MAF sensor/IAT sensor No.1, verify that the MAF sensor output voltage (PID: MAF) varies. (See ON-BOARD DIAGNOSTIC TEST [SKYACTIV-G 2.5].)

  • If not as specified, replace the MAF sensor/IAT sensor No.1. (See MASS AIR FLOW (MAF) SENSOR/INTAKE AIR TEMPERATURE (IAT) SENSOR NO.1 REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [SKYACTIV-G 2.5].)
