2014 - Mazda6 - Transmission/Transaxle

DUAL-MASS FLYWHEEL INSPECTION [C66M-R]

CAUTION:

  • Do not rework the dual-mass flywheel if it is distorted.

  • Do not clean the dual-mass flywheel with any kind of fluid. Clean the dual-mass flywheel with a dry cloth only.

  • Do not clean the gap between the primary and secondary mass. Only clean the bolt connection surface and the clutch surface.

1. Remove the manual transaxle. (See MANUAL TRANSAXLE REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)
2. Remove the clutch cover and clutch disc. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)
3. Rotate the dual-mass flywheel or attempt to move it up and down, and left and right to verify that the center of the dual-mass flywheel does not move.

  • If there is any movement as indicated by the arrows in the figure, replace the dual-mass flywheel. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)

4. Verify that the secondary mass does not rotate by 15 teeth or more.

  • If it rotates by 15 teeth or more, replace the dual-mass flywheel. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)

5. Measure the amount of guide pin projection of the dual-mass flywheel.

  • If it exceeds the maximum amount, replace the dual-mass flywheel. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)

    Dual-mass flywheel guide pin projection maximum amount

  • 11.0—12.0 mm {0.434—0.472 in}

6. Using a dial indicator, measure the dual-mass flywheel runout.

  • If it exceeds the maximum runout, replace the dual-mass flywheel. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)

    Dual-mass flywheel maximum runout

  • 1.5 mm {0.059 in}

7. Remove the dual-mass flywheel. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)
8. Visually inspect the dual-mass flywheel for crack.

  • If there is any crack, replace the dual-mass flywheel. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)

9. Visually inspect the ring gear on the dual-mass flywheel for damage.

  • If there is any damage, replace the dual-mass flywheel. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)

10. Visually inspect the surface that contacts the clutch disc for scratches, nicks, and discoloration.

  • If there is any malfunction, replace the dual-mass flywheel. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)

NOTE:

  • Correct slight scratches and discoloration using sandpaper.

11. Visually inspect for grease leakage between the primary mass and secondary mass.

  • If there is grease leakage, replace the dual-mass flywheel. (See CLUTCH UNIT REMOVAL/INSTALLATION [C66M-R].)
