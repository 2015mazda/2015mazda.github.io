2014 - Mazda6 - Engine

MECHANICAL [SKYACTIV-G 2.5]

Outline

  • The sliding resistance*1 has been reduced by:

    • Adoption of a rocker arm (built into needle roller bearing)

    • Reducing load on the valve spring

    • Narrowing down the crankshaft journal

    • Optimizing the piston skirt shape

    • Adoption of a low-tension piston ring

    • Lowered tensioning of the drive belt

  • A maintenance-free design for valve clearance has been achieved with the adoption of the HLA.

  • With the adoption of a piston cavity, cooling loss has been reduced.

  • The pumping loss*2 has been reduced with the adoption of the variable valve timing mechanism on both sides of the intake and exhaust.

  • The timing chain behavior has been stabilized and sliding resistance has been reduced by optimizing the shape and rigidity of the timing chain-related parts.

*1
Resistance (friction force) which occurs when two object slide against each other. The larger the sliding resistance, the greater the energy loss.
*2
Energy loss which occurs from each type of resistance corresponding to intake and exhaust is called pumping loss.
